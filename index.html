How Every Session is Structured

We follow a 4-step method in every session. No child is ever just sitting and listening.

1
Hook — A Shocking Question
Every session opens with a question that shocks students into thinking. "Does someone sit inside a traffic signal and change it manually?" — They think they know. They don't. Curiosity opens the brain.
⚡ Engagement First
2
Reveal — Real World Explanation
We reveal the actual answer with animated demos and visuals. We connect every concept to something the student has personally used — Zomato, Instagram, Free Fire, ATMs. Never abstract. Always personal.
🧠 Deep Understanding
3
Apply — Student Answers First
Before we explain, we ask students to guess. Wrong answers are celebrated — because a wrong guess means the brain was trying. We then show why the right answer makes sense. This is how memory forms.
🎯 Active Learning
4
Connect — "Where Else Does This Happen?"
We end every concept by asking "where else have you seen this?" — A student who can connect one concept to three others has truly learned it. This is what exam toppers naturally do. We teach it explicitly.

The "Iceberg" — Our First Big Lesson

We teach students that every technology has two parts — what you SEE and what is actually HAPPENING. This one concept changes how they think about everything.

📱 WhatsApp Message Sent ✅
  • ✓
    End-to-end encryption kicks in — your message is converted into unreadable code before leaving your phone
  • ✓
    Data packets created — the message is broken into small pieces and each travels a different internet path
  • ✓
    Meta's server in Singapore receives it — the server verifies both accounts and routes the message
  • ✓
    FCM notification sent — Google's Firebase Cloud Messaging wakes up the recipient's phone
  • ✓
    Message reassembled and decrypted — only on the recipient's device. Not even WhatsApp can read it.

We Teach Business Thinking Through Stories

On Day 2, your child learns how real companies were born — not from theory, but from real frustration. Select a company to see how we teach it.

🍕
Zomato
Started 2008 · Worth ₹60,000+ Crore today
😣 The Problem Deepinder Saw
Office cafeteria had paper menus. Colleagues wasted 15 minutes every lunch hour just trying to figure out where to eat or what was available. This happened every single day.
"What if ALL restaurant menus were available online, on one website? No more paper. No more calling. Just open and decide."
✅ What He Did
He scanned restaurant menus and uploaded them to a website called "Foodiebay." No delivery at first — just information. People loved it. He had accidentally found a real problem.
📌 What we teach children from this
Great companies don't start with big plans. They start with someone getting frustrated and deciding to fix it. Your child can do the same.
🚕
Ola
Started 2010 · Operates in 250+ Indian cities
😣 The Moment That Changed Everything
Bhavish Aggarwal booked a cab in 2010. The driver stopped midway and demanded extra money. When Bhavish refused — the driver left him stranded on the highway. Alone.
"Why is this system so broken and unaccountable? What if the cab booking was fixed price, GPS-tracked, and the driver was rated by passengers?"
✅ Ola's India-First Thinking
Unlike Uber (which came from the US and was card-only), Ola understood India. Cash payments. Auto-rickshaws. Hindi-speaking support. Tier-2 city expansion first. Local knowledge beat global money.
📌 What we teach children from this
Knowing your customer better than your competitor is more powerful than having more money. Understanding people is a skill. And it can be learned.
🌍
Uber
Started 2009 · San Francisco · Global giant
😣 The Problem Travis Saw
Paris, 2008. Snowy night. Travis Kalanick and his friend couldn't find a cab for over an hour — despite being in a major world city. That frustration became a $70 billion idea.
"What if you could press one button on your phone and a car would appear at your location?"
✅ Why Ola Beat Uber in India
Uber assumed India worked like the USA — cards, English, premium pricing. Ola knew India — cash, autos, regional languages, small-city focus. Same idea. Better local knowledge. Different outcome.
📌 What we teach children from this
A great idea is not enough. You need to understand the people you're building it for. Context is everything. This is critical thinking in real life.
